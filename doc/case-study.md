1. Title: 
Swarly Subtitle: An AI Carnatic music teacher for absolute beginners — built by someone who needed one. One line: "No guru available. No app that actually taught. So I built the teacher I never had." 

2. THE PROBLEM Most people who want to learn Carnatic music face three walls: - No accessible teacher - Apps that assume prior knowledge - Feedback that judges instead of guides ("67% accuracy" means nothing to a beginner) The real gap: nothing that teaches the way a real guru would — starting from breath and posture, building slowly, explaining why before asking you to do. 

3. THE INSIGHT (product thinking section) Key insight that shaped every decision: "Every voice is different. The app should meet the user where their voice is — not ask them to match a fixed pitch." This led to: zero-mean DTW shape comparison (compares pitch contour, not absolute Hz), teacher-style feedback (physical guidance, not percentages), and no calibration (replaced with Silero VAD + Parselmouth for honest voice detection). 

4. PRODUCT DECISIONS (the PM thinking section — most important) Show 5 key decisions as cards, each with: DECISION → ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED → WHY THIS ONE Decision 1: Sa fixed at C4 for ear training Alternatives: Let user pick their Sa, multiple pitch options Why: Beginners given options get confused and second-guess themselves every session. A teacher sets the sruti and says "match this." Simplicity over flexibility here. Decision 2: Harmonium samples over synthesized tones Alternatives: Sine wave synthesis, TTS voice, AI-generated singing Why: Harmonium is what Carnatic teachers actually use in class. Real samples = authentic classroom sound. Synthesized tones sound robotic and undermine trust in the app. Decision 3: Teacher feedback over judge feedback Alternatives: Percentage scores, right/wrong indicators Why: A beginner who sees "23% accuracy" will quit. A beginner who hears "your voice went too high after Ga — relax your throat there" will try again. Decision 4: Score-gated progression (85+ to unlock next lesson) Alternatives: Open access to all lessons, time-based unlock Why: Mirrors real guru teaching — you don't move to Janta Swaras until Sarali is solid. The gate is a feature, not a restriction. Decision 5: Guru's handwritten notation as source of truth Alternatives: Standard music theory textbooks, online sources Why: Every teacher has variations. Using actual class notes from a real teaching session ensures the notation, symbols, and timing rules are exactly as a student would encounter them. 

5. WHAT WAS BUILT (Module 1) Show as a visual 4-section layout: LEARN — Carnatic Lessons Foundation: Breathing & Posture, Finding Your Voice (live register detection), Pitch Awareness (Sa drone + stability line), Ear Training (30 progressive combinations) Curriculum: Sarali Swaras — 13 variations, 3 speeds (kaalams), real harmonium + tanpura drone, Telugu/English notation, live beat highlighting EXERCISES — Carnatic Tests 7-question adaptive test per topic (3 easy, 3 medium, 1 hard) Score-gated: 85+ = ready for next lesson, 90+ = go without hesitation Ear Training Test: hear a swara/combination, identify from 3 options (1 correct, 1 close/adjacent, 1 random — tests real discrimination) SONG COACH Upload any song → AI analysis: register timeline, key moments (where to switch to head voice, where melody jumps), teacher-style walkthrough Practice mode: sing along (song plays softly, live register feedback) Test mode: sing from memory, scored on pitch shape + rhythm + voice ASK Music Q&A chatbot constrained to Carnatic music and singing topics only. Runs locally via Ollama/gemma2:2b. 

6. TECHNICAL APPROACH (brief — this is a PM portfolio not engineering) Show as a simple stack diagram: Frontend: React + TypeScript, Tone.js, Web Audio API, harmonium samples (CC BY 3.0) Backend: FastAPI, Parselmouth/Praat (voice analysis), Silero VAD (noise filtering), librosa, fastdtw (shape comparison) AI: Ollama + gemma2:2b (local, free, no API cost) Audio: Real harmonium samples — nbrosowsky/tonejs-instruments (CC BY 3.0, credited in app) 

7. WHAT I LEARNED 3 honest reflections: - "The hardest product decisions weren't technical — they were about what NOT to build. Calibration, multiple Sa options, AI singing voice — all cut because they'd confuse beginners." - "Building for a domain you're learning yourself is humbling. I had to ask a real teacher why 'Pa ;' sounds the way it does before I could encode it correctly." - "Teacher vs judge is a philosophy, not a feature. Every feedback message had to be rewritten until it sounded like encouragement toward a next step, not a verdict."
